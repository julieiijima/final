Helping the earth

one thrift

at a time.


Production of clothing is very costly. It is often overlooked that enormous amounts of energy are used to create even a simple, cotton shirt. The chemicals used for clothing pollutes the environment. However, it is tragic to know that lots of clothes are being wasted as well. In America, an average of 26 billion pounds of clothes end up in landfills. To create a better future for our next generations, we need to take action now.
As one of the solutions, we encourage everyone to thrift because reusing clothes = reducing waste and pollution.

Let's get to the facts!

13.1 million tons
of textiles are thrown away each year

Every second one garbage truck
of clothes are burned or landfilled

2700 liters of water is used
to make one cotton shirt

Discarded clothing made of non-biodegradable fabrics
can sit in landfills for up to 200 years
